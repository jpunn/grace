Stepping into to their dream lands, youth tend to believe they have landed in fair havens.
  In Acts 27, we see Paul had his worst sailing experiences from fair havens, which continued until he reached Melita island.                                                                          
At least a few aspirants feel similar challenges after reaching the so called “fair havens".

In Acts 27 we see, Paul faced winds of varying intensity as follows

    Wind blowing against (Acts 27:4)
    Wind not allowing (Acts 27:7)
    Gentle blowing wind(Acts 27:13)
    Wind of hurricane force (Acts 27:14)
    Raging Storm (Acts 27:20) Similarly, youth are affected by the effects of the above kind of winds in the foreign land in one way or other.  

     "Wind blowing against": is the one that diverts their attention to worldly thoughts, diminishing their actual vision. They get confused by: the attractions of the foreign land, the ways to settle down, searching for jobs, getting along with new friends, weather, and living conditions. 
     "Wind not allowing" is the peer pressure causing them to take wrong decisions, withdrawing from spiritual activities, reducing church attendance.
     "Gentle blowing wind"- Down the road, things seem to be favorable. Enjoying the freedom of this land, hanging out with friends, earning dollars on their own, make them feel independent.  
     Next comes the "Wind of hurricane" in the form of anxiety or fear of failing exams, fear of losing relationships, anxiety to face reality. Eventually the bright ones earlier, become addicted to various things at their reach which are “legal".
     The final one "Raging Storm" is depression which prepares their mind to do the worst. 

 
Solutions:


    Find a local church which is bible based, worshiping God in true spirit.
    Start reaching the church even before landing the ‘fair havens '.
    Be actively involved in church or youth meetings.
    Maintain a good relationship with pastor or youth leader, seek help whenever required, open up your concerns.
     “Talk for yourself “
    Never compromise faith
    Be surrounded with faith friends and gain confidence to say No to evil.
    Stand for Christian values, holiness.
    Be transparent to parents. Talk to them daily. 
    Avoid getting into traps set by wolves such as money lending.
    Know the priorities of life- Keep God first. 
    True heroes stand up for what they believe

2 Timothy1:7 "For God has not given us a spirit of timidity, but of power and love and discipline.
