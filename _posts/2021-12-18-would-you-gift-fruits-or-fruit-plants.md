Gift of prayer for a true blessing:

We all pray for one another. Scripture encourage us to do so. Praying for someone means interceding for their needs before God. We earnestly wish to help them and bring their needs to the powerful almighty God so that they will be blessed. 

At least a few people expect others to pray for them in their needs, however, do not wish to walk in the Lord. Isn't it better to guide them to have a relationship with our Heavenly father so that they will be confident to pray for themselves. I mean, Guiding them to the gift of God-the eternal life in Christ Jesus. It is like giving a fruit plant instead of fruits. If we grow a tree at our home, it’s easy to pluck the fruits at any time we need it. Having faith in Christ Jesus enables one to have access to the throne at any time. 

 You might get your prayer needs answered through some one who intercedes for you. The faith of the prayer warriors increase with the answers received, while you miss that golden chance to taste Jesus. Apparently, our Lord desires everyone to experience Him and grow in faith through the hurdles of life until you reach heaven. Another reason  is, the person you depend for prayer may fall from the sight of the Lord at any moment. 

Every time Jesus did a miracle, He was trying to gain their faith in God. From the scripture we learn that Jesus had compassion to people who are suffering with physical or mental ailments. Nonetheless, he was deeply moved by their spiritual poverty. He spent more time to teach them about Lord's kingdom and took every effort to increase their faith. 

Likewise, our Father wants us to taste Him, understand the power of His glory, through our prayer needs. God delights to hear from our humble prayer and He loves our presence. Just as we earthly parents wish our kids to be around and talking to us. James 4:2"You do not have because you do not ask God." 

Furthermore, losing eternal life in Christ is the worst thing a person can have after having an encounter with Christ or after receiving all the earthly blessings.

John 5:24
"Truly, truly, I say to you, whoever hears my word and believes Him who sent me has eternal life. He does not come into judgment, but has passed from death to life. "

 Hence, when we pray for someone’s needs, be mindful to gain their faith in Christ without which they are not TRULY blessed. 

                TRUE BLESSING IS ETERNAL LIFE WITH CHRIST JESUS
